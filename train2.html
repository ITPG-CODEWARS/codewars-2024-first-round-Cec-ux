Overview:

The Train à Grande Vitesse (TGV) is France's high-speed rail system, launched in 1981. It connects major cities across France and into neighboring countries like Belgium, Switzerland, and Spain. The TGV is well-known for its innovation in rail technology and continues to be a crucial part of Europe’s high-speed rail network. The TGV has held several world speed records for conventional trains.
